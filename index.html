We have developed several different instruments for imaging the human retina at a cellular scale. One class of instrument uses adaptive optics to correct the temporally varying, higher-order aberrations of the eye. Another class of instrument uses interferometry to detect faint reflections from cells that would otherwise make them invisible in the living eye. These instruments have been used to study normal and diseased retinas and optic nerves.
